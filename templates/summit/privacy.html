Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard the personal information you provide when registering for and participating in our Summit and related services, in compliance with the Constitution of Kenya (2010) and the Data Protection Act (2019).

1. Introduction

Your privacy is protected under Article 31 of the Constitution of Kenya, 2010, which guarantees every individual the right to privacy. We also comply with the provisions of the Data Protection Act, 2019, which regulates how personal data is collected, processed, stored, and shared in Kenya.

2. Information We Collect

When you register for the Summit, we collect the following information:

  • Title, First name, and Other names
  • Email address and phone number
  • Organization and Institution name
  • Job title or role
  • Areas of professional or personal interest
  • Other information you choose to provide (such as ‚ÄúOther Interests‚Äù)

We may also automatically collect technical information (such as browser type, device, and usage statistics) to help us improve the website and user experience.

3. How We Use Your Information

Your information is used to:

  • Process and confirm your event registration
  • Communicate event updates, reminders, and logistical details
  • Personalize your Summit experience based on your areas of interest
  • Facilitate networking and professional engagement opportunities (only with your consent)
  • Improve our services and plan for future events

4. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell or rent your personal information. We may share your information only under the following circumstances:

  • With your consent: For example, sharing your professional profile with other participants to facilitate networking if you agree.
  • With service providers: Such as email communication platforms, event management tools, or payment processors, strictly for the purposes of delivering event services.
  • Legal compliance: If required to comply with applicable law, court orders, or regulations, including the Data Protection Act (2019).

5. Data Security

We adopt reasonable organizational and technical measures to protect your personal data, including:

  • Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) encryption for data transmission
  • Restricted access to personal information to authorized personnel only
  • Regular review of our information storage and processing systems

6. Your Rights

In line with the Constitution of Kenya (2010) and the Data Protection Act (2019), you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Request deletion of your information once it is no longer necessary
  • Object to or restrict the processing of your personal data
  • Withdraw consent for communications or marketing
  • Lodge a complaint with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC) if your data rights are violated

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy. Data may be retained for record-keeping, compliance with legal obligations, or to inform you about future Summit events (if you have consented). Once the retention period ends, your information will be securely deleted.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect legal, operational, or service-related changes. Any updates will be posted on this page with the effective date revised accordingly. Continued use of our services after such updates indicates your acceptance.

9. Compliance with Kenyan Law

Our data practices are guided by:

  • Article 31 of the Constitution of Kenya (2010): which protects your right to privacy.
  • The Data Protection Act, 2019: which sets out principles of data protection, rights of data subjects, and obligations for data controllers and processors.
