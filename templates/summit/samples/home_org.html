Background & Context

{% endcomment %}

The global economy is being transformed by software development, artificial intelligence (AI), cloud technologies, and digital entrepreneurship. Kenya, with its dynamic youth population, thriving ICT sector, and fast-growing digital economy, is strategically positioned to lead Africa's next wave of digital transformation.

The Kenya Software Summit aligns with Kenya's vision of decentralizing innovation and fostering regional digital economies.

Our Vision

  • Become Africa's leading annual software and digital innovation conference by 2030
  • Cement Kenya's leadership as Africa's "Code Nation"
  • Support Kenya's ambition to create 1 million digital economy jobs by 2030
  • Position Kenya as a global partner in AI innovation and software exports

Summit Objectives

Build Software Ecosystem

Strengthen Kenya's software development ecosystem, positioning the country as Africa's premier hub for software innovation.

Develop Talent

Develop software and AI talent, providing direct linkages between Kenyan developers and global technology players.

Connect Innovators

Connect emerging innovators, startups, and students with local and international companies, accelerators, and venture funds.

Speakers

Hon. William Kabogo Gitau

Cabinet Secretary, Ministry of ICT & Digital Economy (MICDE), Government of the Republic of Kenya

Hon. William Kabogo Gitau is the pioneer Governor of Kiambu County, a role in which he dedicated his visionary leadership and business acumen, leading to the recognition of the County as the second-best performing county in revenue collection in the Financial Year 2016-2017. Prior to being elected Governor, Hon. Kabogo had served as Member of Parliament for Juja Constituency for two terms, Chief Executive Officer for Toto Industries, and an auditor in the Office of the Auditor General.
